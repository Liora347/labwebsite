# Research

The Meshorer Lab focuses on understanding how chromatin and epigenetic mechanisms regulate cellular identity, development, and disease.

---

## Epigenetic Regulation and Chromatin Dynamics

Embryonic stem cells exhibit highly dynamic chromatin structure.  
We investigate how chromatin plasticity contributes to pluripotency and differentiation.

Key topics:
- Nuclear organization  
- Histone dynamics  
- Chromatin remodeling  

---

## Stem Cells and Differentiation

We study how stem cells transition between states and how epigenetic mechanisms regulate lineage commitment.

---

## Neurodegenerative Disease Modeling

Using human induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs), we model diseases such as:

- Huntington’s disease  
- Machado-Joseph disease  

We aim to understand early molecular changes in disease progression.

---

## Paleo-epigenetics

We develop computational methods to reconstruct DNA methylation patterns from ancient genomes.

This allows us to study:
- Gene regulation in archaic humans  
- Evolutionary differences between species  

---

## Methods

Our lab integrates:
- Stem cell culture  
- Epigenomics (DNA methylation, chromatin profiling)  
- Live imaging  
- Computational biology
